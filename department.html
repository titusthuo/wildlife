WHERE WE BEGAN

Amboseli was set aside as the Southern Reserve for the Maasai in 1906, but returned to local control as a game reserve in 1948. Gazetted a national park in 1974 to protect the core of this unique ecosystem.

PROTECTED AREAS BY THE KWS

Amboseli National Park

Amboseli National Park, situated in the heart of Kenya, is one of the country's most iconic and picturesque protected areas. Renowned for its breathtaking views of Mount Kilimanjaro, Africa's highest peak, Amboseli offers visitors a unique blend of spectacular scenery and abundant wildlife. The park encompasses diverse habitats ranging from expansive grasslands to acacia woodlands and seasonal swamps, providing a sanctuary for a wide array of animal and plant species. Home to iconic African wildlife such as elephants, lions, cheetahs, giraffes, zebras, and wildebeests, Amboseli offers unparalleled opportunities for safari adventures and wildlife encounters. Visitors to Amboseli National Park can enjoy thrilling game drives, guided walks, and cultural experiences while immersing themselves in the natural beauty and rich biodiversity of this world-renowned wilderness area.

Lake Amboseli

Lake Amboseli, nestled within the expansive Amboseli National Park in Kenya, serves as a vital lifeline for both wildlife and local communities. This picturesque lake, fed by seasonal rivers and underground springs, forms a central feature of the park's landscape and ecosystem. It provides a critical source of water for Amboseli's diverse array of wildlife, attracting herds of elephants, buffalo, and other animals during the dry season. Moreover, Lake Amboseli serves as a breeding ground for waterfowl and supports a rich diversity of birdlife, including pelicans, flamingos, and numerous species of waders and raptors. Beyond its ecological significance, Lake Amboseli also holds cultural importance for the Maasai people, who have lived alongside its shores for centuries and continue to rely on its resources for their livelihoods and traditions.
